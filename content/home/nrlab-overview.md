<div class="nrlab-section-intro">
  <p class="nrlab-eyebrow">What NRLab Studies</p>
  <h2>We do not reduce symptoms to a single score. We connect behavior, brain networks, model parameters, and clinical outcomes into an evidence chain.</h2>
</div>

<div class="nrlab-two-col">
  <div>
    <p>NRLab focuses on addiction, digital behavior, eating and obesity, adolescent mental health, psychiatric symptoms, and neuromodulation interventions. Many questions begin with daily experience: gaming that becomes difficult to stop, cues that repeatedly trigger craving, social media evaluation pressure, or unstable behavioral trajectories after treatment.</p>
    <p>The lab does not stop at description. Behavioral tasks record choices, scales capture symptoms and craving, fMRI and EEG describe brain networks, computational models compress choice trajectories into parameters, and interventions such as rTMS, tDCS, MST, focused ultrasound, and TI place targets, brain signals, symptom change, and long-term outcomes into one testable chain.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="nrlab-note">
    <h3>Core Statement</h3>
    <p>We enter science through human behavioral difficulties and return with research paths that are measurable, modelable, intervention-ready, and reproducible.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="nrlab-chain">
  <div><span>01</span><strong>Behavioral Problems</strong><p>Craving, impulsivity, sleep, social stress, and digital behavior.</p></div>
  <div><span>02</span><strong>Measurement Tasks</strong><p>Scales, behavioral tasks, cue reactivity, and longitudinal tracking.</p></div>
  <div><span>03</span><strong>Brain and Models</strong><p>fMRI, EEG, network analysis, machine learning, and computational models.</p></div>
  <div><span>04</span><strong>Translational Intervention</strong><p>Neuromodulation, RCTs, response prediction, and follow-up outcomes.</p></div>
</div>
